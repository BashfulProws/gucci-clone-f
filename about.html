The House of Gucci

Founded in Florence in 1921, Gucci is one of the world's leading luxury fashion brands.

1921

A Florentine Legacy

In the heart of Florence, a city long celebrated for its artistic mastery and dedication to fine materials, Guccio Gucci established a small workshop devoted to leather goods of exceptional quality. Drawing upon the refined sensibilities he had absorbed during his years working among the grand hotels of London, he envisioned a house where the elegance of British aristocratic style would meet the unrivalled skill of Tuscan artisans.

From its earliest days, the atelier gained a reputation for selecting only the most distinguished hides and employing time-honoured techniques passed down through generations of Florentine craftspeople. Each piece that left the workshop bore the unmistakable mark of patience and precision — hand-stitched seams, carefully burnished edges, and hardware forged to last a lifetime. This commitment to excellence laid the foundation for a name that would come to represent the very pinnacle of Italian luxury.

Today

The Art of Craftsmanship

More than a century after its founding, the house continues to honour the artisanal traditions that defined its origins. Within dedicated ateliers across Italy, skilled craftspeople shape, stitch, and finish each creation by hand, guided by an intimate understanding of materials that can only be developed over years of practice. Rare leathers, fine silks, and precious metals are transformed through techniques that remain largely unchanged from those practised by the earliest generation of the house's artisans.

Yet tradition alone does not define the spirit of the maison. A restless curiosity drives constant exploration — new silhouettes emerge from unexpected material pairings, sustainable innovations reshape production processes, and digital craftsmanship opens frontiers that the founder could never have imagined. It is this dialogue between heritage and forward-thinking design that ensures each collection feels both rooted in history and entirely of its moment.

Our Values

Craftsmanship

Every detail is considered, every stitch intentional. Our artisans devote meticulous attention to each creation, ensuring that the highest standards of quality are met from concept to completion.

Innovation

We honour tradition while pushing boundaries. Through bold experimentation with materials, techniques, and creative vision, we continually redefine what luxury means for a new generation.

Sustainability

We are committed to responsible practices that protect the planet. From ethically sourced materials to reduced-impact production, sustainability guides our decisions at every step of the journey.

Inclusivity

We celebrate the beauty of diversity in all its forms. Our house welcomes every individual, embracing different cultures, perspectives, and self-expression as the very essence of modern luxury.

Key Moments

1921

Founded in Florence by Guccio Gucci, the house opens its first workshop dedicated to fine leather goods and artisanal craftsmanship.

1938

Opened the Rome boutique on Via Condotti, bringing Florentine elegance to the capital and marking the beginning of national expansion.

1953

The bamboo handle bag debuts, becoming an instant icon and establishing the house's reputation for inventive design rooted in natural materials.

1966

The Flora print is created — a vibrant botanical motif that would become one of the most recognisable patterns in luxury fashion history.

1994

A new creative era begins, ushering in a bold reinvention of the house that redefines modern luxury for a global audience.
